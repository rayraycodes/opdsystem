Design a marketing landing page for a “World-Class Patient-Centered Hospital Appointment & Live Queue Tracking System” built for high-volume South Asian OPDs (AIIMS, Bir Hospital, Apollo/Medicity chains). This is not a generic health app landing page. The page must tell a credible story of OPD chaos transformed into calm, measurable order.

AUDIENCE
Primary: Hospital leadership (CEO/COO/Medical Superintendent), OPD heads, nursing leadership, quality and patient experience teams, IT and data teams.
Secondary: Government stakeholders and donors, private chain operations heads, insurance partners.
Tertiary: Patient advocacy groups.

BRAND POSITIONING
Brand promise: “From crowded queues to guided care.”
Tone: calm, authoritative, grounded in real OPD realities. No hype. Show empathy for patients and respect for staff.
Brand keywords: Trust, Clarity, Flow, Equity, Dignity, Reliability.

VISUAL IDENTITY (TOKENIZED)
Colors (accessible contrast, AAA where possible):
- Primary: Trust Blue (authority and clarity)
- Accent: Healing Teal (calm and care)
- Neutral warm grays for content-heavy sections
- Semantic colors: success, warning, danger, info (all with text labels, not color-only)
Typography:
- Choose a highly legible sans-serif that supports Devanagari and Nepali scripts well.
- Use large default body size (16–18) with generous line-height.
- Include “Elder Mode” preview option (larger type scale).
Illustration/Photography style:
- Realistic, documentary-style hospital scenes, not glossy stock photos.
- Use simple diagrams to show patient flow and queue logic.
Iconography:
- Clear, universal icons with labels, high contrast, no thin strokes.

PAGE GOALS
1) Communicate the OPD problem in South Asia with specificity (crowding, multiple queues, confusion, emergency interruptions, shared phones, low literacy, low connectivity).
2) Show the solution in a patient-centered + staff-centered way.
3) Prove impact with measurable outcomes: reduced crowding, improved wait transparency, higher throughput, improved accessibility, better staff coordination.
4) Explain how it integrates with ABHA (India) and is adaptable for Nepal’s evolving Health ID ecosystem.
5) Convert: “Request a demo”, “Talk to an implementation specialist”, “Download the pilot playbook”.

LAYOUT AND GRID
- Web landing page: 1440 width, 12-column grid, max content width 1120, spacing system 8pt.
- Mobile responsive version with stacked sections and sticky CTA.
- Add a sticky top nav with section anchors and a persistent “Request Demo” CTA.

LANDING PAGE SECTIONS (HIGH FIDELITY WIREFRAME CONTENT)
1) Hero Section: “OPD Chaos to Calm”
- Headline: emphasize order, dignity, predictable waiting.
- Subheadline: real-time token tracking, guided navigation, assisted booking, offline-first reliability.
- Primary CTA: Request Demo
- Secondary CTA: See How It Works (scroll to flow)
- Hero visual: split-screen before/after
  - Before: crowded waiting room, confusion
  - After: clear token ETA ranges, staggered arrivals, calm flow

2) Problem Section: “Why OPDs Break”
Use bold, specific bullets with small illustrations:
- Multiple lines: registration, vitals, consult, labs, billing
- Walk-ins and referrals collide with booked appointments
- Emergency cases interrupt schedules
- Patients arrive too early and crowd because they fear missing their turn
- Illiteracy and shared phones make app-only systems fail
- Connectivity drops inside campuses cause breakdowns
Include a short “day in the life” story of an elderly patient and a nurse.

3) Solution Overview: “A System, Not Just an App”
Three pillars with icons:
A) Patient Experience Layer
- Multilingual (Hindi/Nepali/English), caregiver booking, IVR/SMS support
- Live queue tracking with ETA ranges and arrival guidance
- Campus wayfinding with landmark steps
B) Staff Operations Layer
- Front desk registration, triage, doctor console, supervisor dashboard
- Emergency interrupt controls and buffer-time logic
- Real-time updates that drive patient notifications
C) Reliability + Interoperability Layer
- Offline-first caching and sync
- Event timeline audit trail
- ABHA integration readiness and pluggable health ID design for Nepal

4) “How It Works” Workflow (Visual)
Show a step-by-step flow diagram:
- Book (assisted or self) → Receive token + arrival window → Navigate to clinic → Check-in scan → Live queue updates → Consult → Follow-up scheduling.
Add a small callout: “Time window, not false precision.”

5) Key Features Grid (8–12 feature cards)
Each card must include: title, short description, and who benefits (Patient/Staff/Both).
Must include these features:
- Assisted booking and family wallet
- Low-bandwidth mode and offline slip
- Real-time queue with ETA ranges and “Now Serving”
- Emergency buffer and interrupt events
- Doctor credentials and transparent fee ranges
- Landmark-based campus navigation + accessible routes
- No-show and late policy handling with recovery
- Supervisor dashboard with bottleneck heatmap
- Audit timeline and compliance logs
- Multilingual accessibility with Devanagari/Nepali support

6) Accessibility and Equity Section: “Built for the Elderly and the Underserved”
Make accessibility concrete:
- Large touch targets, high contrast modes, voice guidance
- Clear language and icon + text redundancy
- Support for assisted journeys and low literacy
- Script-safe typography and spacing for Devanagari/Nepali
- WCAG 2.1 AAA intent: explain what it means in practice (readability, navigation, predictable behavior)
Include a mini checklist with checkmarks.

7) Impact Metrics Section (Quantified Outcomes)
Create metric cards with realistic, operational language. Example metrics:
- Reduced waiting room crowding by staggering arrivals
- Higher OPD throughput via reduced friction between stages
- Lower no-show rates via timed reminders and clearer expectations
- Reduced staff interruptions: fewer “where do I go” questions
- Improved patient satisfaction due to transparency and dignity
Add a “Measurement & Reporting” note: dashboards and audit trails enable continuous improvement.

8) Integration and Trust Section
- ABHA (India) readiness: link identity progressively, consent-aware portability positioning
- Nepal readiness: pluggable Health ID layer and MPI matching approach
- Security and privacy: role-based access, audit logs, minimal patient data exposure in public displays
Include trust badges and simple explanations, not legal jargon.

9) Testimonials / Stories Section (Credible, not marketing fluff)
Write 3 short story cards:
- Elderly patient who avoided hours of waiting
- Nurse who reduced crowd control and focused on care
- OPD supervisor who regained operational control
Use realistic names and context, avoid exaggerated claims.

10) Implementation Section: “Pilot to Rollout”
Show a phased timeline:
- Week 0–2: discovery, workflow mapping, signage alignment
- Week 3–6: OPD pilot (one department)
- Week 7–12: expand to multiple departments + labs/pharmacy integration
Add what hospitals need: roster data, clinic locations, basic fee rules, counter workflows.

11) Final CTA Section
- Strong CTA: Request Demo
- Secondary: Download Pilot Playbook
- Contact options: phone/email plus “Schedule walkthrough”
Include reassurance: “Works with assisted booking and low connectivity.”

INTERACTION DETAILS
- Sticky nav with anchors: Problem, Solution, How It Works, Features, Accessibility, Impact, Integrations, Implementation
- A “See Live Queue Demo” interactive micro module: animated token counter and ETA range
- Toggle demo for “Elder Mode” and “High Contrast”
- Language toggle preview (Hindi/Nepali/English) to show script support

DELIVERABLES IN FIGMA
- One full landing page (desktop)
- Mobile responsive version
- Design system tokens for brand colors and type scale
- Components: feature cards, metric cards, testimonial cards, timeline, CTA blocks, nav, toggle controls
- Content should be fully written (no lorem ipsum) and rooted in OPD realities and process transformation.

COPYWRITING CONSTRAINTS
- No generic claims like “seamless” or “revolutionary” without specifics.
- Always tie benefits to OPD processes: registration, vitals, consult, lab, billing, pharmacy, navigation, emergency handling.
- Emphasize dignity, clarity, and operational control.
- Keep language simple and translatable.
